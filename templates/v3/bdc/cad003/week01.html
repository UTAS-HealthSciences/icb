This module

This module we are working on really getting comfortable with using MyLO and on your emerging skills with paragraph writing. If you need to, go slowly, read and re-read and write and re-write. It is important to remember that this unit is the first of many units in this course. Whilst the skills you will develop during this semester will serve you well in this unit it is important for you to remember that I see this as a continuing process for the duration of this degree. Some skills will "come" relatively quickly, others will take longer, but the important thing is to "pace" yourself. It is tempting, indeed admirable, to spend much more time in the first few weeks of a unit only to discover that sustaining such a workload is difficult. Conversely, it is all too easy to not engage with the material in the first few weeks and to suddenly realise that the workload has indeed increased and that your skill set has not reached the necessary level to complete tutorial tasks or indeed assignments. For this, and many other reasons, I strongly suggest that you read the Cottrell text (see below) for it gives valuable and important advice and more importantly the tools needed for a successful student at university level. The excerpt (chapter 1) should give you enough material to read until you purchase a copy of the text. This is the only text that needs to be purchased by you. The remainder of our texts are all digital and will require you to learn how to access and utilise them.

For those of you studying CAD001, a unit that will indeed be extremely important for you in terms of accessing materials to ensure a successful level of engagement, you will notice a number of new and exciting tools and concepts presented to you. I will also cover some of the material presented in the first few weeks of CAD 001 however this is mainly for the benefit of those who are not enrolled in that unit and/or I consider to be valuable enough to show you in the context of this unit.You will notice that in module 2 (week 3) the amount of time you need to spend with the tutorial materials will increase.

Layout, format & MyLO overview

This is introduced in CAD001, if you have already done/are already doing CAD001 please disregard this section.

Please be aware that these lessons are designed for someone who is both new to MyLO and to UTas - this means that some of you, depending on your computer skills levels, may be much faster than your peers. Remember that we are a community of scholars and at some time we will each rely on others for our learning.

What will usually happen is that you will be directed to some content - a video or a reading. This will sometimes be done with links where you have to click and will be taken to the content. At other times, for the readings, you will be directed to your reading list. After you have viewed or read the content and taken notes, come back to this page and do the next task. This will often be a discussion.There will always be a list labelled 'to do this week'; use this as a guide to ensure that you have covered all the content for the unit.

Web browsers- it is important for you to have more than one web browser on your computer (Internet Explorer, Mozilla Firefox, Safari, Google Chrome are all web browsers). Sometimes MyLO does not work well with a browser and the first trouble shooting activity should be to select a different browser. Always start with Google Chrome as MyLO tends to work well on this browser.

To get a browser, go to the Internet using your usual browser and search for Internet browser download. Select one of the browsers and follow the instructions to download it to your computer.

Information on the bars- mini and nav bars -The mini-bar is the blue bar across the top of your MyLO page. It is always visible. If you scroll down a page, it stays at the top. It allows you to navigate to all of your units and to your own MyLO areas such as your eportfolio, mail and your personal information. Spend some time looking at each of the items on this bar. If you get lost, going to the left of the bar and selecting home will always return you to your first MyLO page.The navbar is the grey bar it is the bar which is important for you to navigate within each unit. It is important to navigate to tutorial material using content and then navigating to learning space. This way, you will be able to systematically work through each tutorial.

Discussion

 
 

As a result of what you have read in the reading this week (Cottrell pp 9-23), what do you see as the most important aspects of tertiary study you need to keep in mind as you begin your journey in the BDemCare? Brainstorm a list and then rate it in order of importance. Select the top one and write a paragraph explaining why it is important. Write this paragraph on the discussion board.

Here is a link to the discussion board.

After you have written your paragraph, go back and have a look at a few of the other responses. Respond to one of these either:

  • adding more reasons why you think your peer's choice of aspect of being a tertiary student is important
  • responding positively to your peer's post - if, for example you had not thought of their particular outlook
  • suggesting a way to go about becoming successful in the aspect the other student has described.
