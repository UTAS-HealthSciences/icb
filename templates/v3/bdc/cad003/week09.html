Lecture

 
 

Academic reading and study skills are primarily based on comprehension. If one doesn't comprehend the given material it then becomes a near impossible task. I cannot emphasise the importance of building your comprehension skills. The YouTube clip and the lecture which deals with note-making and note-taking is directly related to this. While watching the video and the lecture below consider the following:

  • Do you take notes during a lecture or video presentation? If not, then I suggest you begin, now.
  • What types of notes do you take? Are you a list maker or a diagram maker? Perhaps you are a comination of the two?
  • Have you been "taught" to make notes before studying this course?
  • Where else could you use note-taking?
This lecture is also a response to the summary assignment. It was evident in the marking that a number of students were not yet comfortable with paraphrasing. This is a vital skill not only for the last assignment, but for the remainder of this degree.
