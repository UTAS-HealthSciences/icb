This module

This module we are continuing with working with your emerging skills with paragraph writing.

Reading

 
 

There are two readings for week 3. The first is the third chapter of Kirton and McMillan (2006) from the electronic reading list. You can find the reading list on the right-hand side of the first page of CAD003. Make sure you look under the Texts tab. The second is also from the reading list and it is chapter 2 of Tredinnick (2006) The little red writing book.

As you undertake the reading, pay attention to the method suggested to skim and read topic sentences, this is explained in the lecture.

The second reading by Tredinnick is an easy read. The author comes from a literary background and whilst the text is not strictly academic text his ideas regarding sentences and how these are created through the use of creative examples is an insightful and enjoyable experience. The imagery he uses to explain aspects of language use and style is relevant for your understanding of the writing process.

Discussion

 
 

As a result of what you have viewed in the lectures and read in the readings this week I would like you to do the following:

From the lecture about linking sentences:

  1. Reflect on the process of planning for the description of family member, friend, or colleague. Include what worked and what didn't work. Write what you found easy about the task. Also, include the difficulties you faced.
  2. Have you planned in this manner before? What are the advantages and disadvantages of planning in this manner? Do you tend to plan in a list form? What are the advantages and disadvantages of planning in this manner?

From the lecture about topic sentences from Kirton and McMillan (2006)

  1. The actual process. The experience. What worked, what didn't etc.
  2. Your thoughts about the content of the chapter.
