Copy to clipboard button not working?

Some recent updates to both Flash media player (which the copy-to-clipboard button relies upon), and Chrome may be affecting the copy-to-clipboard button for some people.

These updates combine together in a couple of different ways, and you may have to try several of the solutions below before the copy-to-clipboard button works again.

 

What has happened

There are three (I think) types of 'Flash' that are in common use:

  • Flash can be installed directly on your computer
  • A 'Flash plugin' may be installed in your browser
  • Chrome has just introduced its own built-in version of Flash in it's latest browser update.

There are various settings that your browser may use:

  • Your browser may always be allowed to run Flash wherever it encounters it.
  • Your browser might ask you each time whether or not you want to allow Flash to run
  • Your browser might allow you to run "Important Flash Content" 
  • Your browser might allow you to run Flash on particular websites that you've told your browser to trust.

Not all combinations of settings and types of Flash play nicely with the copy to clipboard button.

In general - we recommend that you install the latest version of Flash on your computer, and set your browser to use the installed version of Flash, rather than a plug-in or the new built-in Flash native to Chrome.

 

Making the button work again

First: Update your browser to the latest version, then restart your computer. (In some cases this will fix the problem).

Second: Install the latest version of the Flash player on your computer (if you can).

You can download the latest version of Flash from the Adobe Flash website at: https://get.adobe.com/flashplayer/. You can download and install Flash for free. Make sure you unselect the optional offers (McAffee Security and TrueKey) before you download - you don't want those!

Third: Set your browser to use the installed version of Flash, and not the plug-in or native version of Flash. 
 

 

For Chrome:

For Chrome:

Type into the address bar (the place where you would usually type a web address), the following, exactly as it appears (no extra 'http://' or 'www' etc.) and press 'enter'.

chrome://plugins

Scroll down to the section labelled 'Adobe Flash Player' and UNTICK  the 'Always allowed to run' setting.  This will stop Chrome using a plugin or it's built-in version of Flash, and use the version you have installed on your computer.

Now type into the address bar the following:

chrome://settings

Scroll to the bottom of the settings and follow the 'Show advanced settings...' link. This will extend the page, showing more settings. 

The first collection of extra settings is headed 'Privacy'.  Just under this heading is a 'Content settings…' button. Click it. 

This will open a dialog box. Scroll down the dialog box to the 'Flash' section.

You will be shown three choices here, but the middle choice varies a little – it will either say:

  • 'Detect and run important Flash content (recommended)'
    or;
  • 'Ask when a site wants to use Flash'

If you are offered the 'Detect and run' option, selecting it should fix the problem. 

If you are offered the 'Ask when a site wants to use Flash' option, you have two choices:

  • You could choose 'Allow sites to run Flash', but that is less secure. 
  • Alternatively you can Click the 'Manage exceptions...' button and add both of the following domains to the exceptions sites, with the 'Allow' permission:
https://health.utas.edu.au
https://mylo.utas.edu.au

Click 'done' on the open dialog boxes to set the permissions.

That should fix the problem.

  

For browsers other than Chrome

For browsers other than Chrome

Don't. Use Chrome instead.

 

"I did all that and the copy button still doesn't work!" and/or
"I can't install Flash on my computer"

We have had reports of cases where the copy-to-clipboard didn't work for someone who was using the installed version of Flash, rather than the plug-in. The solution in that case was to allow the plug-in to run. i.e follow the instructions in the "For Chrome" box, but TICK the 'Always allowed to run' setting.  The same advice applies to people who do not have permission to install Flash.
