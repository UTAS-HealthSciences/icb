Critical thinking means weighing up the arguments and evidence for and against. Edward Glaser who developed a test of critical thinking defined it in this way (1941): Critical thinking calls for persistent effort to examine any belief or supposed form of knowledge in the light of the evidence that supports the further conclusions to which it tends”
(Cottrell 2013, p. 187).

Module 4

Critical thinking is probably what you already do when you read a newspaper. You question what you read and often take what you read with a ‘pinch of salt’. There is often good reason for this. However, when it comes to other sources many do not question what has been written or presented.
