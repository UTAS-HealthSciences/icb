Reflect upon the different components of this flow diagram and their importance in developing strategies to improve outcomes for people with dementia.

Modify environment to minimize frustration

Environment Strategies:

  • Appropriate level of stimulation
  • Assess noise level (turn off TV/radio unless resident clearly engaged)
  • Appropriate temperature/clothing
  • Examine effect of other residents’ behaviours

Caregiving Strategies:

  • Adequate rest periods
  • Timing of care
  • Most appropriate caregiver
  • Simplify instructions (provide clear explanation, one step instructions)
  • Ensure wearing hearing aids/glasses if needed
  • Cue resident with situation appropriate objects
  • Enable resident to perform the ADLs that he/she can perform Distract resident during care (e.g. music, conversation about topic of interest)
  • Redirect resident away from situation (guide by arm, smile, introduce new activity)
  • Regular toileting (initiate/review)

Modify resident’s program to engage resident

Programming Strategies:

  • Activities to engage resident (Snoezelen room, recreation therapy, music/art therapy, picture books, massage, pet visits, walks, puzzles etc.)
  • Timing of activities.
